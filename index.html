Understanding GST & Tax Compliance

Goods and Services Tax (GST) and Income Tax laws in India involve multiple rules, procedures and compliance timelines. Non-compliance can result in penalties, interest and legal issues. Proper understanding and timely compliance help businesses avoid unnecessary risks and ensure smooth operations.

This website is designed as an informational guide to help you understand GST registration, return filing, income tax obligations and TDS requirements in a simple and structured manner.

GST Registration

GST registration is mandatory for businesses whose turnover exceeds prescribed limits or who fall under compulsory registration categories. Once registered, businesses are required to comply with GST return filing and payment rules.

Different types of GST registration are available depending on the nature and scale of business operations.

GST Return Filing

GST returns are periodic statements that contain details of outward supplies, inward supplies, tax liability and input tax credit. Filing returns on time is mandatory even if there are no transactions during a period.

Different GST returns apply based on the type of taxpayer and registration.

Income Tax & TDS Compliance

Income Tax laws apply to individuals, firms and companies earning income in India. Filing income tax returns within due dates is mandatory and failure may result in penalties and interest.

Tax Deducted at Source (TDS) applies to specified payments such as salary, rent, professional fees and contractor payments, and requires periodic return filing.

GST, Income Tax and TDS laws prescribe specific due dates for filing returns and making tax payments. Missing due dates can lead to late fees, interest and other legal consequences.
