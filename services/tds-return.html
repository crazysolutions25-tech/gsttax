TDS Return – Complete Guide

Detailed information on Tax Deducted at Source (TDS), applicability, types of returns, due dates, penalties and compliance rules.

1. What is TDS?

Tax Deducted at Source (TDS) is a mechanism under the Income Tax Act where tax is deducted at the time of making specified payments such as salary, rent, professional fees, interest and contractor payments. The deducted tax is deposited with the government on behalf of the payee.

2. Who is Required to Deduct TDS?

  • Employers paying salary
  • Businesses making specified payments
  • Companies, firms and LLPs
  • Individuals and HUFs subject to audit
  • Government and statutory bodies

3. What is TDS Return?

A TDS Return is a quarterly statement submitted to the Income Tax Department containing details of TDS deducted, deposited and credited to the payee. Filing TDS returns is mandatory for all deductors.

4. Types of TDS Returns

➤ Form 24Q

Filed for TDS deducted on salary payments.

➤ Form 26Q

Filed for TDS deducted on payments other than salary, such as professional fees, rent and contractor payments.

➤ Form 27Q

Filed for TDS deducted on payments made to non-residents.

➤ Form 27EQ

Filed for Tax Collected at Source (TCS).

5. TDS Return Filing Due Dates

Quarter Period Due Date
Q1 April – June 31st July
Q2 July – September 31st October
Q3 October – December 31st January
Q4 January – March 31st May

6. Documents Required for TDS Return Filing

  • TAN of the deductor
  • PAN details of deductor and deductees
  • Challan details (BSR code, challan number)
  • Details of payments and deductions

7. Late Fees for TDS Return Filing

Late filing of TDS return attracts a fee under section 234E:

  • ₹200 per day for delay
  • Maximum late fee limited to the amount of TDS

8. Penalty for Non-Filing or Incorrect Filing

  • Penalty ranging from ₹10,000 to ₹1,00,000 under section 271H
  • Interest on delayed deposit of TDS
  • Disallowance of expenses in income tax assessment

9. Consequences of Non-Compliance

  • Demand notices from Income Tax Department
  • Loss of credibility
  • Legal proceedings in severe cases

10. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q: Is TDS return filing mandatory?
Yes, every deductor who deducts TDS must file quarterly returns.

Q: Can TDS return be revised?
Yes, corrections can be filed to rectify errors.

Q: What happens if TDS is deducted but not deposited?
Interest and penalties will be levied.
