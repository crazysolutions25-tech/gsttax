Income Tax Return – Complete Guide

A detailed guide on income tax return filing, eligibility, types of returns, due dates, penalties and compliance rules.

1. What is Income Tax Return (ITR)?

An Income Tax Return (ITR) is a form through which a taxpayer reports income earned during a financial year to the Income Tax Department. It contains details of income, deductions, exemptions, tax paid and refund claimed, if any.

2. Who is Required to File Income Tax Return?

  • Individuals whose total income exceeds the basic exemption limit
  • Businesses and professionals earning taxable income
  • Companies, firms and LLPs (mandatory filing)
  • Individuals claiming tax refunds
  • Persons with foreign assets or foreign income

3. Basic Exemption Limits

Category Basic Exemption Limit
Individuals (Below 60 years) ₹2,50,000
Senior Citizens (60–80 years) ₹3,00,000
Super Senior Citizens (Above 80 years) ₹5,00,000

4. Types of Income Tax Returns

➤ ITR-1 (Sahaj)

Applicable for salaried individuals having income from salary, one house property and other sources (excluding lottery).

➤ ITR-2

Applicable for individuals and HUFs having income other than business or profession.

➤ ITR-3

Applicable for individuals and HUFs having income from business or profession.

➤ ITR-4 (Sugam)

Applicable for presumptive income under sections 44AD, 44ADA and 44AE.

5. Income Tax Return Due Dates

Category Due Date
Individuals (Non-Audit) 31st July
Businesses (Audit cases) 31st October
Transfer Pricing cases 30th November

6. Documents Required for ITR Filing

  • PAN Card
  • Aadhaar Card
  • Form 16 / Salary slips
  • Bank statements
  • Investment proof (80C, 80D, etc.)

7. Late Fees for ITR Filing

Late filing of income tax return attracts a fee under section 234F:

  • ₹5,000 if filed after due date
  • ₹1,000 if total income does not exceed ₹5 lakh

8. Interest on Late Payment

Interest may be charged under sections 234A, 234B and 234C for delay in filing return or payment of advance tax.

9. Penalties for Non-Filing of ITR

  • Loss of carry forward of losses
  • Penalty and interest
  • Prosecution in extreme cases

10. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q: Is filing ITR mandatory?
Yes, if income exceeds exemption limit or in specified cases.

Q: Can I file a belated return?
Yes, but with late fees and interest.

Q: Can I revise my ITR?
Yes, returns can be revised within the allowed time.
